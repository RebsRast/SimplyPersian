Sauces & Liquids

Pomegranate Juice

Some say it tastes like grape juice. Which may be true but the fresh pomegranate juice is a bit more sour - depending on the picked fruit.

Benefits

  1. Vitamin C
  2. Dietary fiber
  3. Common cold
  4. Skin care

Pomegranate Paste

Pomegranate paste, not to be confused with pomegranate molasses, which is a thin and brown. Best used in persian stews and some bar cocktails like grenadine.

Benefits

  1. Vitamin C
  2. Dietary fiber
  3. Common cold
  4. Skin care

Rosewater

This fragrant liquid is mainly used in desserts. Remember this pungent liquid is packed with flavor - a little goes a long way.

Benefits

  1. Ease depression (think aromatherapy
  2. Cleanse skin

Sour Grape Juice

No mystery here; it tastes exactly how it sounds. This juice is mainly added to soups, meat, and chicken stocks to add flavor and tartness to dishes.

Benefits

  1. Anti-coagulant
  2. Anti-inflammation
  3. Cleanses the bladder
  4. Anti-carcinogenic
  5. Mild laxative
  6. Improves eyesight/vision

Tamarind Paste

Cumin seeds are pungent, potent little things with the ability to significantly change the trajectory of a dish.

This paste is used in many stews to add a tart flavor. The paste contains the pulp surrounding the seeds of the tamarind pod.

Benefits

  1. Dietary fibers
  2. Antioxidant (tartaric acid)
  3. Good source of minerals
